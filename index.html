What to expect: Hot, sunny, and humid — classic late-July Jamaica. Expect brief afternoon or evening showers (usually 20–30 min) that clear fast. Mornings are almost always clear. Rain is warm so it won't ruin the day. Hurricane season is technically active (Jun–Nov) but direct-hit risk in July is historically under 1%. Ocean is warm and calm — perfect for water activities. Drink double your normal water intake.

Arbiter Protocol

Jamaica Meal Plan

Pescatarian · 160g protein · 50g net carbs · 80–100g fat · Active recovery all week · You have access to 15+ restaurants across Ocean Eden Bay + Ocean Coral Spring.

Restaurant Guide — What's Approved & What to Order
Restaurant Best Compliant Order Avoid
Marlin Beach
Seafood · Buffet + À La Carte		 Grilled fish or seafood à la carte at dinner. Buffet breakfast: eggs, steamed veg. Avoid buffet lunch — too many starch traps. Rice, jerk sauces with sugar, fried anything, festival bread, plantain
Captain's Market
International Buffet		 Eggs + veggies at breakfast. Dinner: grilled protein station + steamed/roasted veg. Always build your plate at protein + veg stations. Pasta, rice, potatoes, bread rolls, dessert station, gravies
Bluemoon
International À La Carte		 Grilled fish or seafood entree. Ask for veg side instead of starch. Sauce on the side always. Cream sauces, pasta dishes, bread basket
Red Snapper ⭐
Coral Spring · Beach Seafood		 Grilled whole fish, steamed fish, shrimp. Ask for steamed cabbage or veg side. Best lunch spot on the resort. Fried dumpling, festival, rice and peas, bammy
Sugar Cane
Coral Spring · Jamaican		 Steamed fish, escovitch fish, callaloo eggs. Ask for no rice. Steamed cabbage approved. Jerk fish if not too saucy. Oxtail, rice and peas, plantains, festival, hard dough bread
La Locanda
Coral Spring · Italian		 Grilled branzino or seafood. Ask for sautéed veg instead of pasta. Skip the bread. Pasta, risotto, pizza, cream sauces
Sakura
Coral Spring · Teppanyaki		 Grilled shrimp, scallops, or fish off the teppanyaki grill. Ask for no rice, extra veg instead. Soy sauce OK. Rice, noodles, teriyaki sauce
Jamaican Grill
Coral Spring · Jerk Outdoor		 Jerk fish if available — ask for light sauce or on the side. Pair with steamed cabbage. Jerk chicken/pork, rice and peas, fried plantain
Thali
Coral Spring · Indian		 Tandoori fish or grilled seafood if available. Cucumber raita on side. Most curries OK protein-wise — watch the rice. Rice, naan, roti, sugary chutneys
Steak House
American À La Carte		 Skip — meat-focused. If you go, ask if they have fish. Otherwise pass. Everything — not pescatarian friendly

Always Ask For

"Can I get grilled instead of fried?" — always ask this first

"Can I get steamed vegetables instead of rice/potatoes?" — resorts always have this

"Can I get the sauce on the side?" — every restaurant

"Is there a fish option on the menu?" — at Steak House or Route 66 if you end up there

"Do you have steamed cabbage or callaloo?" — Jamaican restaurants will always have these

Always Available at Buffets

✓ Scrambled or fried eggs — unlimited, high protein

✓ Steamed or sautéed vegetables — mushrooms, zucchini, broccoli

✓ Grilled fish or seafood at dinner stations

✓ Callaloo (Jamaican leafy green) — approved, high protein plant side

✓ Steamed cabbage — approved, great side dish

Hard Avoids the Whole Trip

✗ Rice and peas — everywhere in Jamaica, always say no

✗ Festival bread, bammy, hard dough bread

✗ Fried plantain — fruit, off limits

✗ Fruit at the buffet — no matter how fresh it looks

✗ Cocktails and alcohol

✗ Jerk sauces — usually contain brown sugar; ask for light or on the side

✗ Dessert station — skip entirely

Drink Strategy

✓ Water constantly — double your 64oz target in Jamaica heat

✓ Plain sparkling water from the bar

✓ Plain coffee or tea — unsweetened, Stevia drops if you brought them

✓ Zevia if you packed it — worth throwing a few cans in your bag

✗ Cocktails, rum punch, tropical juices

✗ Sports drinks, coconut water

Pack in Your Bag

✓ Sweetleaf Stevia drops — for coffee at the resort

✓ Protein powder (ISO 100) — single serve packets for breakfast or snack

✓ 5–7 ONE Bars — for travel days and snack gaps

✓ Zevia cans — a few for pool days

✓ Small bottle of Frank's Red Hot — optional but great for bland resort food
